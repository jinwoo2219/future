New Enemy Role System V1

1. Purpose
- This document defines the head-approved enemy role and movement baseline for the new project.
- New enemies should use these shared categories before unique gimmicks are added.

2. Enemy Role Categories
- frontline
  - standard lane pressure that wants to reach the player directly
- runner
  - lower durability, faster arrival pressure
- tank
  - higher durability, heavier breach threat
- disruptor
  - changes lane shape, pushes, pulls, or interferes with formation
- support
  - buffs, protects, or improves other enemies rather than being the main body itself
- artillery
  - creates delayed pressure, backline pressure, or row-based pressure
- summoner
  - adds more enemies or expands board pressure over time
- totem
  - fixed pressure source or field-effect source
- boss
  - phase-based or centerpiece threat

3. Current Movement Pattern Id Baseline
- straight_1
  - moves downward by 1 each enemy movement step
- straight_2
  - moves downward by 2 each enemy movement step
- side_shift
  - may change lane as part of its movement rule
- hold_then_move
  - waits or charges before advancing
- static_support
  - does not advance normally and acts as a support/structure piece

4. First-Pass Rule
- New enemy creation should first pick:
  - one role category
  - one move_pattern_id
- Do not invent new categories or movement ids unless the existing list clearly fails.

5. Starter Mapping
- Basic:
  - category: frontline
  - move_pattern_id: straight_1
- Speeder:
  - category: runner
  - move_pattern_id: straight_2
- Tank:
  - category: tank
  - move_pattern_id: straight_1

6. Design Rule
- Role should describe what pressure the enemy creates.
- move_pattern_id should describe how that pressure reaches the player.
- Unique gimmicks should be layered on top of a readable base role.

7. Approval Note
- This is the current head-approved baseline for enemy role and movement categorization.
